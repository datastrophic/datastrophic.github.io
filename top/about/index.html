datastrophic

Hi! It’s Anton here and I’m the author of datastrophic.io - my personal blog. I’m a technical leader and a software engineer specializing on distributed systems, data platforms, and AI infrastructure.

So what does datastrophic mean at all?

The name originates from circa 2015 when I was helping startups to design their in-house data platforms and we talked a lot about various issues related but not limited to data loss, consistency, delivery semantics, idempotent writes, backups etc. and the impact they might have on the business. Not surprisingly, quite often a breach of SLAs, or an irrecoverable data loss were having catastrophic consequenses.

datastrophic (adj.) - description of a catastrophic situation originated in a data platform that resulted in a severe damage. Architecture drawbacks, technology choices, insufficient scalability (or lack thereof) are some of many reasons that lead to a datastrophic event.

Although there’s no specific theme in this blog its main focus is on architecture design and knowledge sharing. Most likely the majority of recent blog posts will be related to Kubeflow and Kubernetes. Prior to Kubernetes, it was mostly about Spark and Mesos but time flies fast.
